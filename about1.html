About Microplastics in Our Food Chain

Microplastics are tiny plastic particles that pose significant risks to our health and the environment.

Sources of Microplastics

They originate from:

  • Breakdown of larger plastic items
  • Washing synthetic textiles
  • Personal care products

Health Risks

Microplastics can accumulate in the human body, leading to potential health risks such as:

  • Inflammation
  • Hormonal disruptions
  • Potential carcinogenic effects

The dangers of microplastics


Microplastics are the fragments of stabilizers, lubricants, fillers, plasticizers, and other chemicals that manufacturers use to give plastics their desirable propertiesTrusted Source, such as transparency, flexibility, and durability. However, experts have classified many of these chemicals as toxic and harmful to human health.
