Microplastics in Our Food Chain

Microplastics are a growing concern in our environment, affecting ecosystems and human health.

What are Microplastics?

Microplastics are small plastic particles less than 5mm in size, originating from various sources, including the breakdown of larger plastic items and synthetic fibers.

Impact on Food Chain

Microplastics can enter the food chain through:

  • Contaminated water sources
  • Seafood consumption
  • Soil contamination affecting crops

What do we know about microplastics in food?


Most of the plastics produced globally are used for food and beverage packaging. During its use, however, plastic becomes worn and breaks into small fragments called microplastics. In this Honest Nutrition Feature, we explain the potential dangers of microplastics, how food becomes contaminated with them, and ways to reduce exposure.
